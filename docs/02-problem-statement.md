# Tuition Management Platform - Problem Statement

## Context
Private tutoring is a key component of the Bangladeshi education system. University students rely on tuition for income, while parents seek tutors to support their children's education.

## Problem Details
1. **Inefficient Matching:** Tutors and guardians have no centralized directory. Finding matches takes days or weeks.
2. **Scams and Fraud:** Fake tutors apply with forged credentials, and fake guardians lure students into unsafe situations.
3. **Exploitative Commissions:** Matching agencies charge up to 100% of the first month's salary, putting a heavy financial burden on student tutors.

## Impact
- Guardians lose money and compromise their children's safety.
- Qualified tutors remain unemployed or receive underpaid jobs due to intermediary exploitation.
- High transaction friction degrades trust in the tutoring system.

## Gap Analysis
- **Current State:** Fragmented, risky, and expensive matching using social media and offline agents.
- **Desired State:** A free/low-cost, authenticated platform where verified tutors directly connect with guardians, complete with ratings and reviews.
